About

Rachel Saunders is an activist for environmental and social justice, empowered by using GIS and geospatial technologies. Her career began at Stetson University where she obtained her BS in Environmental Science. While she attended Stetson, she worked with the Center for Community Engagement as the Marketing Outreach Director. A major issue that faced the DeLand community was rooted deep within the town’s past of slavery and white power. Where today, minority groups still feel excluded from the main downtown area, an area that has white imperialist paintings displayed. Although these paintings are historical and represent the history of the town, they also promote an invisible fear and persecution that remains hidden within cracks of the buildings and the roads. In her position she worked closely with her supervisors to create a new dialogue that discussed the history of the town and issues that they still face today; bringing to light racism, sexism, and oppression so that conversation can be created to work towards creating a more inclusive future.

Rachel’s passion for the environment manifested inside of her at a very young age. Growing up she frequently visited her marine biologist uncle who opened her eyes to a world both under the sea and above. One where the interconnectivity of all living things created a seamless and harmonious balance. In college she explored these intricacies through both her education and field experience. Studying everything from sea level rise to politics she discovered that her passion was in her voice and GIS. She believes that analytical maps display thought provoking visualizations that allow her to create bridges of dialogue between both sides of the conversation, allowing for healthy debate and disagreements.

Currently, she is working on her MS in Geospatial Technologies at the University of Washington Tacoma where she hopes to further her passion’s in social and environmental justice.
